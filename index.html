Exotic dance moves

Yes, you are right! As part of my post-doctorate work, I did study exotic tribal dances. To answer your question, my favourite dances are as follows, with definitions:

Polynesian chicken dance:
A little known but very influential dance involving waving your hands around like a chicken. It was originally performed in Polynesian islands, but is now popular worldwide.

Icelandic brownian shuffle:
Before the Icelanders developed the art of modern dance, they used to practice this dance, which involved a lot of shuffling around in circles. Scientists say this was a precursor to the modern Brownian motion discovered by Einstein in the early 1900s.

Arctic robot dance:
An interesting example of ancient influences on modern robotics. English explorers in the 1960s believed to have discovered a previously unknown tribe “by robots”, studied movements being practiced by inhabitants of Northern Alaska and Canada. Later on however it was discovered that they were just moving like this because it was really cold.
